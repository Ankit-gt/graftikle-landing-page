DHI Technique

Direct Hair Implantation (DHI) is an advanced hair transplant technique that uses a specialized tool called a Choi Implanter Pen to directly implant hair follicles into the scalp without the need for pre-made recipient sites.

No need for recipient site creation

Minimal trauma to the scalp

SAPHIRE FUE

Sapphire FUE (Follicular Unit Extraction) is an advanced hair transplant technique that uses specialized sapphire blades instead of traditional steel tools to create micro-incisions in the scalp for hair follicle implantation. This method is designed to offer more precise, smaller incisions, leading to faster healing, reduced scarring, and more natural-looking results compared to standard FUE procedures

More precise, smaller incisions

Faster healing and reduced scarring

More natural-looking results

Frequently Asked Questions

Common Questions About Hair Transplants

Get answers to the most common questions about our procedures and services

How long does a hair transplant procedure take?

The duration of a hair transplant procedure depends on the technique used and the number of grafts needed. Typically, a session can last between 4 to 8 hours. For larger areas or higher graft counts, we might recommend splitting the procedure into multiple sessions for optimal results.

Is a hair transplant procedure painful?

We use local anesthesia to ensure your comfort during the procedure. Most patients report minimal discomfort during the treatment. After the procedure, you might experience some mild soreness or tenderness in the donor and recipient areas, which typically subsides within a few days and can be managed with prescribed pain medication.

How long does it take to see results after a hair transplant?

Hair growth after a transplant follows a timeline: the transplanted hair will fall out within 2-3 weeks (this is normal), and new growth typically begins around 3-4 months after the procedure. Most patients see significant results by 6-9 months, with full results visible around 12-18 months post-procedure.

What's the difference between FUE, DHI, and Sapphire FUE techniques?

FUE (Follicular Unit Extraction) involves extracting individual hair follicles and transplanting them to the recipient area. DHI (Direct Hair Implantation) uses a specialized tool to extract and implant follicles in one step, allowing for more precise placement and denser results. Sapphire FUE uses sapphire blades instead of steel, resulting in smaller incisions, faster healing, and more natural-looking results.

Are hair transplant results permanent?

Yes, hair transplant results are generally permanent because the transplanted hair follicles are taken from areas of the scalp that are resistant to the hormone that causes hair loss. However, it's important to note that existing hair (not the transplanted hair) may continue to thin over time. We offer comprehensive post-procedure care and maintenance plans to help maintain your results long-term.
